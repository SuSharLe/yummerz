Ingredients

  • Elbow macaroni
  • Butter
  • Milk
  • Garlic Powder
  • Cheddar Cheese
  • Grated Parmesan cheese
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Paprika

Instructions

  • Cook the Pasta: Boil the macaroni according to package directions, drain, and set aside.
  • Make the Roux: In a large pot, melt butter over medium heat and then add the flour and whisk to form a smooth paste.
  • Add Milk: Slowly pour in the milk, whisking constantly, until the mixture thickens.
  • Add Cheese: Stir in the shredded cheddar and Parmesan, and keep stirring until the cheese melts and the sauce is smooth.
  • Season: Add salt, pepper, and any additional seasonings.
  • Combine: Toss the cooked pasta into the cheese sauce until fully coated.

Reminders

  • Do Not Overcook the Pasta: Make sure to cook the pasta al dente.
  • Gradually Add Milk: When adding the milk to the roux, pour it in slowly and whisk constantly.
  • Season to Taste: Don’t forget to taste your sauce as you go!
  • Avoid Overheating the Cheese: When adding cheese to the sauce, don’t overheat the mixture. Stir the cheese in off the heat to prevent it from becoming stringy or separating.
  • Bake with Caution: If baking your mac and cheese, bake just long enough for the cheese to melt and the top to turn golden brown.
