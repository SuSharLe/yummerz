Ingredients

  • White Fish Fillets
  • Batter
  • Potatoes
  • Oil
  • Salt
  • Flour

Instructions

  • Prepare the fish fillets: Pat dry your white fish fillets with a paper towel.
  • Make the batter: whisk together 1 cup of flour, 1/4 cup of cornstarch, 1 tsp of baking powder, Salt and pepper to taste, 1 cup of cold water or beer.
  • Coat the fish: Dip each fillet into the batter.
  • Prepare the potatoes: Peel and cut the potatoes into thick chips.
  • Fry the chips: Add the parboiled chips to the hot oil in batches and then fry for about 3-4 minutes.
  • Fry the fish: Lower the battered fish fillets into the hot oil and then fry for about 4-6 minutes.

Reminders

  • Oil Temperature: Make sure your oil stays at a consistent temperature (350°F / 175°C) to avoid soggy fish or chips.
  • Don't Overcrowd the Fryer: Fry the fish and chips in batches, so the oil temperature doesn't drop too much.
  • Drain Well: After frying, let your fish and chips drain on paper towels to remove excess oil. This helps maintain the crispy texture.
  • Seasoning: Season the fish and chips immediately after frying with salt. You can also add vinegar or tartar sauce for extra flavor!
